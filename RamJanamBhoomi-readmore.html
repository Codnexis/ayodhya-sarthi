Ram janam Bhoomi

We are here to assist pilgrims, tourists and travelers visiting the holy city of Ayodhya.

📜 History of Ram Janmabhoomi

Ram Janmabhoomi is the most sacred site in Ayodhya, believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama, the 7th avatar of Lord Vishnu. This is where Maryada Purushottam Ram took birth in Treta Yuga to King Dashrath and Mata Kaushalya.

Why is it called 'Ram Janmabhoomi'?

The word 'Janmabhoomi' means 'Birthplace'. This exact spot is mentioned in Ramayana and Skanda Purana as the birth site of Lord Ram. After centuries of struggle, the grand Ram Mandir was inaugurated here on 22nd January 2024.

Architecture

The present structure is a magnificent Nagara style temple made of Bansi Paharpur pink sandstone. It is 161 feet tall, 3 stories, with 5 mandaps and 392 pillars. The temple complex spans 2.7 acres.

Unique Feature

The sanctum sanctorum houses the 51-inch tall idol of Ram Lalla in his 5-year-old child form. The idol is made of black stone and the temple is designed to last over 1000 years.

Important Instructions for Visitors:

  • Dress Code: Wear traditional Indian clothes. Avoid shorts, sleeveless tops, or revealing clothes.
  • Footwear: Remove shoes/slippers at the designated counter before entering.
  • Entry Pass: Free darshan available, but online booking recommended for special pass.
  • Photography: Mobile phones, cameras and electronic items are strictly prohibited inside.
  • Prasad: Elaichi Dana and Panch Mewa prasad are available at counters.
  • Crowd: Expect heavy crowds daily. Ram Navami is the busiest day. Plan accordingly.
  • Security: 3-tier security check. Carry only wallet and ID. No bags allowed.
  • Cleanliness: Maintain cleanliness. Do not litter in the temple complex.
